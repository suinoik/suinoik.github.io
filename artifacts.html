AInsight Newsletter – Commercial AI Applications Across Three Industries

Objective: To research and present new or updated commercial applications of artificial intelligence across three different industries in a newsletter format that is engaging, accessible, and professionally structured.

Process: This project was completed collaboratively with a team, where we worked together to identify industries that aligned closely with our academic and professional interests. As a group, we selected healthcare, semiconductors, and legal services due to their rapid adoption of AI and real-world relevance. Within this collaborative effort, I contributed to researching commercial AI applications, analyzing their impact on business processes, and developing content for the newsletter. We explored companies such as Abridge and OpenEvidence in healthcare, NVIDIA’s AI-driven semiconductor innovations, and Thomson Reuters CoCounsel in legal technology, focusing on how these tools are actively transforming decision-making and workflows.

Tools: Industry research sources, AI-assisted content development, collaborative planning tools, newsletter design tools, and PDF publishing software.

Value Proposition: This artifact demonstrates my ability to collaborate effectively within a team while contributing meaningful individual analysis. It highlights my skills in researching emerging AI applications, evaluating their business impact across multiple industries, and communicating technical developments in a clear and accessible format. It also reflects my ability to contribute to a polished, professional deliverable that balances teamwork, structure, and analytical depth.

This artifact is a newsletter titled The AInsight, created to examine how artificial intelligence is actively reshaping healthcare, semiconductors, and law. Rather than discussing AI in general terms, this project focuses on practical commercial applications already influencing how organizations operate. The newsletter format allowed us to present these developments in a way that is both informative and engaging for readers who may not have extensive technical knowledge.

In the healthcare section, we explored how Abridge and OpenEvidence are helping reduce administrative burden and support clinical decision-making. These tools show how AI can improve efficiency by assisting with documentation, summarizing medical information, and helping clinicians access evidence more effectively. In the semiconductor section, we focused on NVIDIA’s use of AI in chip design and manufacturing, showing how machine learning is accelerating workflows such as lithography, routing, and timing analysis. In the legal section, we examined Thomson Reuters CoCounsel as an example of AI being used to automate document review, legal research, and workflow support in high-stakes professional environments.

One of the most important insights from this project is that AI is becoming most valuable when it is embedded directly into existing professional workflows. Across all three industries, the common pattern is not simply automation for its own sake, but strategic augmentation. In healthcare, this means giving physicians more time to focus on patients. In semiconductors, it means reducing design time and avoiding expensive errors. In law, it means enabling professionals to complete complex tasks more quickly and at greater scale. This reinforced my understanding that the strongest commercial AI applications are those that improve both efficiency and decision quality.

Working as part of a team also strengthened my ability to collaborate on research-driven projects. It required aligning ideas, dividing responsibilities effectively, and ensuring that our final output maintained a consistent voice and structure. At the same time, I focused on contributing clear analysis and well-structured content to support the overall quality of the newsletter.

Overall, this artifact represents my ability to connect AI innovation with real business transformation while working collaboratively. It demonstrates research, critical analysis, teamwork, and professional communication skills, as well as my ability to evaluate emerging technologies through an industry-specific and practical lens.

AInsight Newsletter – Commercial AI Applications Across Industries

Objective: To research and present new or updated commercial applications of artificial intelligence across three different industries in a newsletter format that is engaging, accessible, and professionally structured.

Process: I reviewed current developments in commercial AI and narrowed my focus to three industries: healthcare, semiconductors, and legal services. I then analyzed specific AI companies and products within each area, including Abridge and OpenEvidence in healthcare, NVIDIA’s AI-driven semiconductor design tools, and Thomson Reuters CoCounsel in legal technology. After identifying how these tools are transforming business processes and decision-making, I organized the findings into a newsletter format with clear sections, visuals, and concise analysis.

Tools: Industry research sources, AI-assisted content development, newsletter design tools, and PDF publishing software.

Value Proposition: This artifact demonstrates my ability to research emerging AI applications, evaluate their business impact across multiple industries, and communicate technical developments in a format that is clear and accessible to a broad audience. It also shows my ability to translate research into a polished, professional deliverable that balances analysis, structure, and visual communication.

This artifact is a newsletter titled The AInsight, created to examine how artificial intelligence is actively reshaping healthcare, semiconductors, and law. Rather than discussing AI in general terms, this project focuses on practical commercial applications already influencing how organizations operate. The newsletter format allowed me to present these developments in a way that is both informative and engaging for readers who may not have extensive technical knowledge.

In the healthcare section, I explored how Abridge and OpenEvidence are helping reduce administrative burden and support clinical decision-making. These tools show how AI can improve efficiency by assisting with documentation, summarizing medical information, and helping clinicians access evidence more effectively. In the semiconductor section, I focused on NVIDIA’s use of AI in chip design and manufacturing, showing how machine learning is accelerating workflows such as lithography, routing, and timing analysis. In the legal section, I examined Thomson Reuters CoCounsel as an example of AI being used to automate document review, legal research, and workflow support in high-stakes professional environments.

One of the most important insights from this project is that AI is becoming most valuable when it is embedded directly into existing professional workflows. Across all three industries, the common pattern is not simply automation for its own sake, but strategic augmentation. In healthcare, this means giving physicians more time to focus on patients. In semiconductors, it means reducing design time and avoiding expensive errors. In law, it means enabling professionals to complete complex tasks more quickly and at greater scale. This helped me understand that the strongest commercial AI applications are the ones that improve both speed and decision quality.

Creating this artifact also strengthened my ability to synthesize current industry developments into a coherent narrative. I had to move beyond simply identifying companies and instead explain why their products matter, how they are being used, and what their future significance may be. The inclusion of visual structure and concise sectioning also reflects my ability to communicate complex ideas clearly and professionally for a broader audience.

Overall, this artifact represents my ability to connect AI innovation with real business transformation. It demonstrates research, critical analysis, written communication, and digital presentation skills, while also showing that I can evaluate emerging technologies through an industry-specific and practical lens.
